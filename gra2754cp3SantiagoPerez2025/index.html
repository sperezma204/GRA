Car exhaust tips DIY

Steps

1. Gather materials

Exhaust tip (stainless steel or chrome, clamp-on style).Wrench or socket set. Metal file or sandpaper. WD-40 (or similar rust penetrant). Gloves and safety glasses

2. Prepare the exhuast pipe

Make sure the car is parked, cool, and off. Inspect the existing tailpipe. Use a wire brush or sandpaper to remove dirt, rust, and carbon buildup so the surface is clean and smooth.

3. Measure and fit

Measure the diameter fo the existing exhaust pipe and the new one. Most clamp on tips come in sizes that slide right over the new one

4. Apply rust pnetrant

If the pipe is rusty or dirty apply a little bit of w 40 and clean it. This prevents the clamp from seizing up and ensures a snug fit.

5. Install the Exhaust Tip

Slide the new exhaust tip onto the existing pipe. Position it so it sticks out slightly beyond the bumper but not too far. Tighten the clamp bolts evenly using your wrench/socket set until the tip is secure and doesnâ€™t wiggle.

6. Let the fun start

Start your car and et it idle for a while so you can check if it is making any sounds, if it rattles, vibrates, or maybe the exhaust has a loose fit. Step back and admire your work. A quick and easy DIY that gives your ride personality and a clean touch.
